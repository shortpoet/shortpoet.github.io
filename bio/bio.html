Born and raised in Manila, Philippines. Mother is Spanish/Italian born and raised in Lima, Peru. Fourth-generation Spanish born in the Philippines to a family with business history. Sa puso Pinoy -- Filipino at heart. Attended a Jesuit school that predominantly serves the Chinese-Filipino community, beginning a life-long pursuit of the Language Arts and Sciences. Took my first baking class at 6, and could bake a baguette by 8. While at boarding school in Ojai, California, nuclear family moved to Madrid, Spain.

Lived with a French family during School Year Abroad program in Rennes, France. Solidified interest and abilities in the gastronomic realm through almost daily preparation of family meals. Continued culinary learning in the professional realm during summers in high school and university. Studied and worked in China for the better part of a year. Acted as the sole cultural and linguistic liaison for a small sales and journalism team, generating the bulk of revenue for the project. Engaged in several successful start-up hospitality ventures, helping develop both the general theme and specific operational details that help engender success. Arrived in Los Angeles for a sojourn, and stayed for a while.

“Not all who wander are lost.”

While searching the soul, I found pieces of the whole. The mother of a very good friend once told me that each of the languages one holds has a personality element tied to cultural references that one balances in their thoughts. The natural curiosity that led me to acquire the moniker of “Mr. Why” as a child, and later on, “wiki,” or even “Kanto Boi,” all stem from an innate desire to explore through communication.

As a 6-year-old, I routinely snuck out of the house in the wee hours to converse with one of the neighborhood security guards in Manila. In my time in Los Angeles, with a bicycle as primary transport, I engaged many a curious creature between bus and bike, during the day, or again in the wee hours of the night. Working as an interpreter, and at times an actor, I left following the call back to family and technology.

“A tree is only as strong as its roots.”

My brother had been working as a software engineer in St. Louis and had been encouraging me to put my linguistic abilities to bear in the pursuit of technology, and relevant machine languages. I had been quite adept as a youngster, dabbling in logo, basic, early internet. A series of events led me to focus elsewhere, but I’ve always loved computing.

A major part of the decision was a longing to be with close family. And through a mix of good fortune and the expected results of a root return, I find myself exploring the fascinating world of coding. I look forward to finding innovative ways to use my talents in unison to deliver technology that helps propel us all into greater enlightenment and oneness with the force.
